# Situation Report - 2026-03-28

## Highlights

**Flashbots Flashnet: TEE-Backed Anonymous Mempools for Ethereum** — Flashbots published the full technical architecture of Flashnet, a low-latency anonymous broadcast protocol for Ethereum's block building pipeline. The design uses TEEs on the client side to enforce honest message preparation, additively homomorphic vector commitments for server-side aggregation proofs, and a threshold model requiring only >50% honest servers. Unlike Tor (weak anonymity) or Nym (high latency), Flashnet achieves strong anonymity against global passive adversaries while maintaining low latency. This directly addresses the identity-leakage gap that onchain privacy tools like Railgun leave open, and positions anonymous mempools as a prerequisite for truly permissionless BuilderNet expansion — a critical Phase 2 piece that requires no Ethereum consensus changes.

**Ethereum Foundation: L1 and L2 as a Unified Scaling System** — The EF's Platform team published a strategic essay that formally repositions L2s as differentiation platforms rather than mere scaling bandaids. L1 scales "by several orders of magnitude" through ZK while remaining the permissionless DeFi settlement hub; L2s are expected to deliver custom sequencing, privacy guarantees, and vertical integration. Key technical asks: Stage 2 rollup status, synchronous composability, native rollups without security councils. This is the most explicit official framing to date of how ETH demand, L2 economics, and shared security are meant to interrelate.

**a16z: Open Agentic Commerce and the Death of Ads** — A well-argued piece drawing the 1990s open-protocol parallel: just as HTTP/DNS beat AOL's walled garden, open machine-payment protocols (x402, MPP) are positioned to displace ad-based models as AI agents replace human browsing. Stack Overflow traffic down 75% since GPT-4, tech news down 60% — cited as evidence that attention-based monetization is collapsing. The MPPScan dashboard (25,654 transactions, $3,710 volume in 7 days across 37 active servers) provides concrete grounding: agentic commerce is not speculative.

**Matthew Green: Anonymous Credentials Primer** — Timely deep-dive into blind signatures and ZK-based anonymous credentials, motivated by 25+ US states enacting age-verification laws. Covers Chaumian blind signatures, NIZK credential proofs, anti-cloning via limited-use credentials and hardware binding, and ZK-based revocation. A clear technical foundation for any privacy-preserving identity system being designed today, and directly relevant to agent authentication infrastructure.

**Post-Quantum Transition Accelerating on Multiple Fronts** — Three independent signals this week: Cloudflare reports client PQ support at >60% (up from <3% two years ago) and origin support at ~10% (up 10x in one year); the TALUS paper delivers threshold ML-DSA (a NIST PQC standard) with one-round signing for validator/MPC contexts; the Ethereum Foundation launched a formal PQ roadmap website. The ecosystem is moving from "research curiosity" to "active deployment" across transport, signing, and application layers simultaneously.

## Blogs & Research

### MEV / PBS / Block Building

**[Network Anonymized Mempools](https://writings.flashbots.net/network-anonymized-mempools)** — Flashbots, Feb 17 2026

Flashnet introduces a three-party anonymous broadcast model: clients run TEEs to produce well-formed encrypted message arrays, servers (threshold >50% honest) aggregate secret-shared arrays using additive homomorphism, and a leader reconstructs and broadcasts to the public mempool. Clients randomly place messages within arrays, making sender-message linkage computationally intractable. Security relies on: (1) client-side TEEs preventing malformed inputs, and (2) additively homomorphic vector commitments allowing servers to prove honest aggregation without revealing individual messages. Compared to Tor (weak anonymity, no liveness) and Nym (high latency), Flashnet achieves strong anonymity against global passive adversaries with low latency. The protocol tolerates partial server failure, fixing ZipNet's single-point-of-failure weakness. Positioned as Phase 2 infrastructure for decentralized block building — no protocol-level changes required. Enables permissionless BuilderNet expansion beyond the current reputation-based operator selection model.

**[Blockchains Are Fast Enough for Finance — Now What?](https://a16zcrypto.substack.com/p/what-blockchains-need-to-compete)** — a16z Crypto, Mar 25 2026

Argues that raw throughput is no longer the bottleneck; *predictability* is. Two unsolved failure modes: censorship (single-leader chains can exclude valid transactions) and information asymmetry (leaders see pending transactions enabling frontrunning). Current DeFi only functions because operators voluntarily restrain exploitation — a social layer that becomes unsustainable as financial stakes grow. Required protocol-level properties: short-term censorship resistance (any transaction reaching an honest node included in next block) and hiding (transaction details invisible before finalization). Applications currently moving critical logic offchain (UniswapX, CoWSwap) to work around these gaps, but this undermines onchain composability.

### Cryptography / ZK

**[Anonymous Credentials: An Illustrated Primer](https://blog.cryptographyengineering.com/2026/03/02/anonymous-credentials-an-illustrated-primer/)** — Matthew Green, Mar 2 2026

Comprehensive walkthrough motivated by the wave of US state age-verification legislation. Covers: blind signature-based single-use credentials (Chaumian model — Issuer signs a message without knowing its contents), NIZK-based multi-show credentials that prove complex properties ("over 25 AND US resident BUT not from NY") without revealing the underlying data, limited-use enforcement via serial numbers, ZK revocation via non-membership proofs, and hardware binding to secure enclaves. Key insight: ZK approaches allow a single credential to power many "show" runs without linkage across uses, solving the efficiency and privacy problems of basic Chaumian schemes. Discusses real-world deployment challenges across device ecosystems including the fragility of hardware binding.

**[Strong Chain Quality](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2026-03-23-strong-chain-quality/)** — Decentralized Thoughts, Mar 23 2026

Introduces Strong Chain Quality (SCQ): an upgrade to traditional Chain Quality that guarantees proportional blockspace inclusion *within each block* rather than only statistically over time. Mechanism: "virtual lanes" allocate per-block blockspace proportional to stake. Implementation requires two additional BFT rounds — validators sign inclusion lists before sending to the leader, and only support blocks containing all signed items. Creates sustainable L1 token demand by making staked blockspace a productive yield-generating asset. Acknowledged limitations: doesn't eliminate proposer "last look" advantages or determine transaction ordering within lanes — ordering remains a separate open problem.

**[Logjumps: A Faster Modular Reduction Algorithm](https://baincapitalcrypto.com/a-deep-dive-into-logjumps-a-faster-modular-reduction-algorithm/)** — Bain Capital Crypto

Yuval Domb's optimization reduces modular reduction over prime fields from n² + n multiplications (Montgomery) to n² + 1. For 256-bit primes on 64-bit systems: 17 vs. 20 multiplications — approximately 15% speedup on a critical hot path. Discovered while optimizing client-side ZK proving. The algorithm decomposes input as H·2^w + c₀, uses a precomputed constant ρ = 2^(-w) mod p, and applies one final Montgomery step, avoiding the full q-computation loop. Field arithmetic dominates ZK proof generation (polynomial commitments, circuit constraint satisfaction), so this directly accelerates proving time. Relevant to any team optimizing client-side SNARK provers.

**[The Limits of Legal Control in Technical Systems](https://leastauthority.com/blog/the-limits-of-legal-control-in-technical-systems/)** — Least Authority, Feb 26 2026

Dissects GDPR's practical failure modes: the right to erasure requires logging deletion requests (generating new data about the erasure), right of access provides disclosure without verification, corrections don't propagate through backups or derived datasets. Core argument: GDPR's accountability model regulates controller *conduct* but doesn't redistribute technical *authority*. Data subjects rely on controller compliance and regulatory enforcement rather than verifiable system control. Privacy requires architectural embedding — E2EE and access controls baked into system design — not just regulatory compliance.

### Privacy

**[How Not to Mandate Device-Based Age Assurance](https://educatedguesswork.org/posts/device-based-age-assurance/)** — Educated Guesswork (Eric Rescorla), Mar 27 2026

Systematic technical critique of California AB 1043, Texas SB 2420, Utah SB 142, and similar legislation. Key problems: DNS-over-HTTPS and Encrypted Client Hello make network-level filtering technically infeasible; open-source software faces impossible compliance burdens; geographic determination requires either location permission overreach or IP-based tracking infrastructure. Critical privacy flaw: deterministic age-range thresholds enable birthday inference attacks — observing a user transition between age brackets on consecutive days reveals their birth date. The legislation also mandates age queries from all apps (weather, calculators) creating unnecessary sensitive data leakage. The author argues that commercially-reasonable standards focused on mobile platforms would be more technically tractable.

### Distributed Systems

**[Consensus Board Game](https://matklad.github.io/2026/03/19/consensus-board-game.html)** — Matklad, Mar 19 2026

Visual exploration of Paxos using a board game metaphor. Key pedagogical contribution: a 2D board model where columns represent concurrent voting rounds and rows represent participants, making multi-round leader rotation and the safety commitment mechanism visually intuitive. Leaders collect majorities who commit to *abstaining* from past columns, preventing conflicting outcomes while ensuring a leader choosing a color based on prior majority commitments guarantees consistency. Useful reference for teaching consensus safety properties.

### P2P / Networking

**[Running Iroh on an ESP32](https://iroh.computer/blog/iroh-on-esp32)** — Iroh, Mar 24 2026

Proof-of-concept running iroh's full QUIC/TLS P2P stack on an ESP32 (4MB flash, ~500KB internal RAM — comparable to a 1992 386). Key engineering solutions: pure-Rust TLS via `rustls-rustcrypto` (ring/AWS-LC incompatible with Xtensa architecture), external PSRAM for dynamic allocation, custom shims for missing Unix symbols (`gethostname` etc.), feature-gated to `TLS13_AES_128_GCM_SHA256` + `X25519` only. The work identifies opportunities to reduce iroh's dependencies for embedded contexts and validates that QUIC-based P2P is feasible on genuinely constrained hardware. Hardware-accelerated crypto would be needed for production use.

## Academic Papers

**[zk-X509: Privacy-Preserving On-Chain Identity from Legacy PKI via ZK Proofs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25190)** — Yeongju Bak, Mar 26 2026

Bridges the ~4 billion deployed X.509 certificates to blockchain identity without new CA infrastructure. Private keys never enter the ZK circuit — ownership is proven via OS keychain signature delegation (macOS Secure Enclave, Windows TPM). Circuit validates: certificate chain validity, temporal correctness, CRL revocation, blockchain address binding, and Sybil-resistant nullifier generation. Commits 13 public values including a CA Merkle root that obscures the issuing authority. Performance: ~11.8M cycles for ECDSA P-256 on SP1 zkVM, ~300K gas for Groth16 on-chain verification. Eight security properties formalized under Dolev-Yao adversary assumptions with reductions to sEUF-CMA and SHA-256 collision resistance. Highly practical: no new credential infrastructure required.

**[TAMI-MPC: TEE-Accelerated MPC for Nonlinear Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24861)** — Li et al., Mar 2026

Combines TEEs with MPC to enable real-time ML inference under resource constraints. Reduces communication rounds and eliminates oblivious transfer bottlenecks — two major practical barriers to MPC-based private inference deployment. Continues the emerging hybrid TEE+MPC architecture pattern also seen in Flashnet.

**[TALUS: Threshold ML-DSA with One-Round Online Signing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22109)** — Leo Kao, Mar 2026

First threshold signature scheme for ML-DSA (Module Lattice Digital Signature Algorithm, a NIST PQC standard) achieving one-round online signing. Addresses carry-propagation and rejection-sampling challenges unique to lattice signatures in threshold settings through boundary clearance and carry elimination preprocessing. Directly relevant to post-quantum threshold signing in validator contexts and MPC committee signing.

**[On the Vulnerability of FHE Computation to Silent Data Corruption](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23253)** — Mar 24 2026

First systematic study of how transient hardware faults (bit flips, cosmic rays, thermal errors) propagate through FHE ciphertext computations. Characterizes vulnerability patterns through fault-injection experiments and evaluates fault-tolerance mechanisms. As FHE moves toward production deployment in cloud confidential computing and private ML, hardware reliability becomes a new correctness and security attack surface distinct from cryptographic assumptions.

**[mmFHE: mmWave Sensing with End-to-End FHE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22437)** — Mar 23 2026

First system applying FHE to radar signal processing. Encrypts sensor data on edge devices; entire processing pipeline runs on untrusted cloud using only ciphertexts. Demonstrates negligible accuracy loss. Signals FHE's maturation from pure cryptography research into applied sensing systems — an important indicator of deployment readiness.

**[Bridges Connecting Encryption Schemes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21694)** — Mar 23 2026

Theoretical study of morphisms between encryption schemes, generalizing bootstrapping procedures used in FHE construction. Proves bridge security reduces to underlying scheme security plus auxiliary assumptions. Contributes to the formal foundations of scheme composition, relevant to hybrid classical/PQC and multi-scheme FHE constructions.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

**TEEs as a Bridge Technology, Not a Trust Anchor**
TEEs appear in multiple distinct roles this week: Flashnet uses client-side TEEs to enforce protocol honesty in anonymous mempools; TAMI-MPC uses TEEs to reduce communication rounds for private ML inference. The pattern is consistent — TEEs are being used not as the *root of trust* but as an *honest-execution enforcement layer* that makes other cryptographic protocols (MPC, ZK) practical at acceptable latency. This hybrid architecture (TEE + MPC/ZK) is emerging as the dominant design for privacy-preserving computation under real-world performance constraints.

**Privacy-Preserving Identity is Converging from Multiple Directions**
Matthew Green's anonymous credentials primer, the zk-X509 arxiv paper, and Least Authority's GDPR limits analysis all circle the same problem from different angles: building identity and authentication systems where users prove *properties* without revealing *data*. Age-verification legislation is accelerating this from theoretical to urgent. zk-X509's leverage of existing PKI infrastructure (4 billion X.509 certs, no new CA required) is a particularly pragmatic shortcut that could see rapid real-world deployment.

**Agentic Commerce Infrastructure is Operational, Not Speculative**
MPPScan's 25K+ transactions/week across 37 servers demonstrates that machine-to-machine micropayments are working now. The a16z "end of ads" thesis is not a forecast — it is a description of an infrastructure stack that already exists: identity (agent registries), payment (x402/MPP), discovery (AgentCash), and compute (Fly.io Sprites/MCP). The question is velocity of adoption, not whether the infrastructure is viable.

**MEV and Censorship Resistance Remain Unsolved at Protocol Level**
Both Flashbots (Flashnet for identity privacy) and a16z (censorship resistance and transaction hiding) this week identify that the social/reputational layer currently suppressing worst-case MEV is insufficient as financial stakes grow. SCQ (Decentralized Thoughts) proposes a protocol-level fix via inclusion lists with stake-proportional virtual lanes. These are complementary pieces: guaranteed fair inclusion requires both identity privacy (Flashnet — who submitted) and protocol-enforced blockspace allocation (SCQ — that inclusion happens). Neither is complete without the other.

**Post-Quantum Transition is Moving Faster Than Expected**
Cloudflare's data shows client-side PQ adoption at 60% in two years from near-zero — faster than TLS 1.3 adoption took. TALUS delivers threshold lattice-based signatures for production signing infrastructure. The EF is formally planning for PQ Ethereum. The remaining gap is origin/server-side adoption (~10%) which is lagging client support by roughly 12-18 months. Teams building long-lived cryptographic infrastructure should be designing for PQ now.
