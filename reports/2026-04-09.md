# Situation Report - 2026-04-09

## Highlights

**1. Boneh: Batch Threshold Encryption Makes Encrypted Mempools Practical** — Boneh, Nema, Roy, and Tas construct an epochless, censorship-resistant batch encryption scheme: linear public parameters, constant-size ciphertexts, constant-size pre-decryption keys. A single group element (via partial fraction decomposition) unlocks an entire batch. CCA-secure and thresholdizable. A threshold committee publishes one small key to decrypt all transactions in a block simultaneously. Censorship resistance means builders cannot selectively exclude transactions from decryption. This is the critical missing piece for real-world MEV protection via encrypted mempools — decryption overhead was the blocker, and Boneh just removed it. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/674)

**2. Verification Facade: Hax Formal Verification Pipeline Has Critical Gaps** — Symbolic Software demonstrates that three classes of semantic gaps in the Hax Rust-to-F\* pipeline (translation infidelity, unverifiable trust boundaries, specification gaming) allow insecure cryptographic code to pass full formal verification. Five PoC exploits target ML-DSA (FIPS 204), ML-KEM (FIPS 203), Ed25519, and ChaCha20 — three pass F\* with "all verification conditions discharged" while containing security-critical flaws. Hax is deployed with Google and tested in Signal's PQ protocol. Formal verification can provide false assurance when translation gaps silently discard security-relevant semantics. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/670)

**3. Perils of Parallelism: Parallel Execution Breaks Transaction Fee Mechanisms** — Wadhwa, Yaish, Zhang, and Nayak prove an impossibility: parallelism-aware fee designs cannot simultaneously protect users from overpaying for unused parallel slots and protect schedulers from revenue loss. Users can insert fake parallel transactions to reduce fees; builders can inject fake sequential dependencies to inflate revenue. Directly relevant to Sui, Monad, and any parallel-execution chain — naive parallelism-aware fee markets open new MEV vectors that don't exist in sequential chains. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/649)

**4. a16z: The Headless Merchant Era** — Noah Levine articulates "headless merchants": businesses with no storefront, no accounts, no sales team — just a server, endpoints, and per-call pricing. MPP saw 894 agents execute 31,000+ transactions ($0.003–$35 each) in its first week. A March marketplace launched 60+ agent-consumable services. "The payment is the authentication" — inverting traditional business development. The shift from commerce as *places* to commerce as *moments* restructures which business models survive in an agent-native economy. [Link](https://a16zcrypto.substack.com/p/entering-the-era-of-the-headless)

**5. VEIL: Zero-Knowledge at 3% Prover Overhead** — Dalal, Hemo, Rabinovich, and Rothblum build a lightweight compiler adding ZK to hash-based multilinear proof systems (Binius-style) by decoupling algebraic interactions from hashing and wrapping only the algebra with ZK. 3% prover overhead, 22% verifier overhead, 12% proof-size overhead on 2^29-element traces. Plausibly post-quantum. ZK rollups using STARKs or Binius can add privacy with negligible performance penalty and no proof-stack redesign. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/683)

## Academic Papers

### MEV / Block Building / Fee Mechanisms

**[Perils of Parallelism: Transaction Fee Mechanisms under Execution Uncertainty](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/649)** — Wadhwa, Yaish, Zhang, Nayak
Impossibility result: cannot simultaneously protect users and schedulers in parallel-execution fee markets. New MEV vectors: fake parallel txns to reduce fees, fake sequential dependencies to inflate revenue. Matching mechanisms at the trade-off boundary. Directly constrains Sui/Monad fee design.

**[Efficient Batch Threshold Encryption Using Partial Fraction Techniques](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/674)** — Boneh, Nema, Roy, Tas
Epochless batch encryption: constant-size ciphertexts and pre-decryption keys. Single group element unlocks entire batch via partial fraction decomposition. CCA-secure, thresholdizable, censorship-resistant. Makes encrypted mempools practical at scale for MEV protection.

**[Braess Paradox in Layer-2 Payment Networks](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/669)** — Kotzer, Rottenstreich
Adding payment channels can paradoxically *increase* routing fees. Formally characterized with empirical validation on real Lightning Network data. Implies topology-aware channel management and fee-aware routing are necessary for efficient L2 scaling — naive channel creation can be counterproductive.

### ZK Proofs / SNARKs

**[VEIL: Lightweight Zero-Knowledge for Hash-Based Multilinear Proof Systems](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/683)** — Dalal, Hemo, Rabinovich, Rothblum
3% prover overhead, 22% verifier overhead, 12% proof-size overhead. Decouples algebraic interactions from cryptographic hashing, wraps only algebra with ZK. Plausibly post-quantum. Makes ZK essentially free on top of STARKs/Binius for rollups needing privacy.

**[Mergeable SNARGs for Trapdoor Languages](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/678)** — Brakerski, Farber Brodsky, Paneth, Solomon
First mergeable SNARGs supporting unbounded polynomial recursive merges with arbitrary topology. Security loss depends only on merge depth (not tree size). Two instantiations: fully compact from sub-exp iO, depth-dependent from LWE. Applications: first CCA1-secure multi-hop FHE and adaptive multi-hop aggregate signatures from LWE. Theoretical backbone for tree-structured proof aggregation in rollups and bridges.

**[Open Problems in List Decoding and Correlated Agreement](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/680)** — Arnon
Reviews open problems from the Ethereum Foundation's Proximity Prize, relevant to succinct proof system design.

**[Efficient Merkle-Tree Consistent Accumulator](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/673)** — Mendonca
Improved accumulator for CT and blockchain state commitments with better proof sizes.

### Consensus

**[SSLE-DAG: High-Throughput PoS Consensus with SSLE](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/647)** — Hladky, Peresini, Mariani, Homoliak
zk-SNARK-based Single Secret Leader Election + adaptive DAG consensus that splits/merges parallel chains on demand. ~1,600 TPS at 40 nodes geo-distributed. Implemented in Go using gnark. Prevents targeted DoS on block proposers while maintaining high throughput.

### Formal Verification / Security

**[Verification Facade: Attacks on Hax Verification Pipeline](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/670)** — Symbolic Software
Three semantic gap classes in Rust-to-F\* translation: translation infidelity (while-loop fuel ghosting), unverifiable trust boundaries (axiomatized ops), specification gaming (`assume!` poisoning). Five PoC exploits pass full F\* verification on ML-DSA, ML-KEM, Ed25519, ChaCha20. Deployed with Google, tested in Signal PQ protocol.

**[SPLASH: Speculative Leakage-Adaptive Secure Hardware](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/677)** — Li, Rostami, Borkar, Wu, Sadeghi
SIFT (Speculative Information Flow Tracking) + reconfigurable speculative table for runtime-configurable control of speculative execution policies. Implemented on BOOM (RISC-V). First post-fabrication, runtime-reconfigurable defense against full spectrum of Spectre-class leakage. Critical for TEEs that must defend against evolving attacks without chip replacement.

### MPC / Privacy

**[FLOSS: Fast Linear Online Secret-Shared Shuffling](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/672)** — Chang, Navot, Ozdemir, Tyagi
New "interactive arithmetic permutation circuits" abstraction. Malicious-secure 2PC with strictly linear online communication for shuffling. Preprocessing model. First linear-online malicious-secure shuffle. Core primitive for private analytics, anonymous communication, and private ad measurement at scale.

**[Robot: Robust Threshold BBS+ in Two Rounds](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/651)** — Tang, Qiu, Jiang, Xue, Yang, Au, Deng, Lam
Two-round (round-minimal) threshold BBS+ with robustness — signing completes with t+1 honest parties. DDH-based threshold VRF for single-round nonce generation. 2.02 KB per-party upload. BBS+ is the foundation of W3C Verifiable Credentials — Robot makes decentralized anonymous credential issuance practical.

**[Fast Perfectly Secure Network-Agnostic MPC](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/655)** — Asharov
Perfect security across both synchronous and asynchronous networks with improved communication and round complexity.

**[FOVA: Verifiable Aggregation for Federated Learning](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/643)** — Zhu
One-shot verifiable secure aggregation addressing gradient inversion attacks via MPC techniques.

### Threshold Signatures

**[The Many Faces of Schnorr: A Touch-Up](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/681)** — Shoup
Tightens random oracle proofs for batch randomness extraction with re-randomization in threshold Schnorr analysis toolkit.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

**Encrypted mempools just became viable.** Boneh's batch threshold encryption provides the missing primitive: a single constant-size key per batch, CCA-secure, censorship-resistant. Combined with the Perils of Parallelism impossibility result (showing parallel execution creates new MEV vectors that sequential chains avoid), the research landscape is simultaneously making MEV protection practical and proving that naive parallelism makes it harder. Protocol designers now have both the tool (Boneh) and the constraint map (Wadhwa et al.).

**Formal verification trust is not transitive.** The Verification Facade paper demonstrates that formally verified code can harbor exploitable bugs when the translation gap between source and proof target discards security-relevant semantics. Three of five PoC exploits pass full F\* verification. This directly affects Hax deployments at Google and Signal's PQ protocol. Meanwhile, SPLASH shows TEE hardware defenses need runtime reconfigurability because fixed-at-fabrication approaches cannot track evolving Spectre-class attacks. Trust boundaries in both software verification and hardware isolation are more porous than assumed.

**Agentic commerce is restructuring, not just growing.** Both dashboards show dramatic shifts: MPP's top service is now HYRE Agent API (didn't exist a week ago), with volume up 390% and agents doubled — but unique servers halved. x402's new entries include MERX and RelAI (institutional-scale facilitator with $17K volume from 25 buyers). The original incumbents (Apollo, ACP-Virtuals) are being displaced. This is no longer early experimentation — it's an ecosystem with real competitive dynamics, consolidation, and wholesale players entering.

**ZK proving approaches practical free privacy.** VEIL achieves 3% prover overhead for adding ZK to hash-based proof systems, and Mergeable SNARGs enable unbounded recursive proof aggregation with security loss only in depth. Combined with the sum-check speedups from last week and Gryphes' modular SNARK architecture, the cost of ZK is collapsing fast enough that "privacy by default" for rollups is becoming an engineering choice rather than a performance sacrifice.
