# Situation Report - 2026-04-12

## Highlights

**1. EF Checkpoint #9: ePBS Is "Trickier Than Anticipated"** — The Ethereum Foundation's protocol support team reports that enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation for Glamsterdam (mid-2026) is proving harder than expected — every part of the stack must reason about partial blocks and two-party coordination. EPBS-devnet-1 is investigating issues; Glamsterdam-devnet-0 was targeted imminently. Hegota (late-2026) selects FOCIL (EIP-7805) for the consensus layer; Account Abstraction downgraded to "Considered" due to implementation disagreement. Gas limit testing targets 60M baseline with higher limits explored. This is the most consequential structural change to Ethereum's block production since the Merge — and the difficulty signals how deeply relay-mediated MEV is embedded in the architecture. [Link](https://blog.ethereum.org/en/2026/04/10/checkpoint-9)

**2. Proximity Signatures: Solving DAS Provenance** — Angeris and Gurkan (Bain Capital Crypto) introduce "proximity signatures" enabling DAS light nodes to verify that sampled data was actually signed by the claimed provider — closing a gap where erasure-coded data could pass proximity checks without provenance. Built on "many-slot signatures" (one valid signature per slot). BLS12-381 instantiation: 48 bytes per column overhead. Complementary to FRIDA and EIP-4844 blob commitments. Light nodes get both data integrity and signer authenticity from a tiny sample. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/694)

**3. HQC Broken via Cache Timing: Compiler Destroys Constant-Time** — Dong and Guo demonstrate the first full decryption oracle key-recovery attack on any PQ cryptosystem. The official AVX2-optimized HQC implementation (NIST standardized, code-based KEM) has constant-time C source, but the compiler silently rewrites mask-based selection into secret-dependent control flow. Flush+Reload extracts predicates from Reed-Muller decoding; GPU-accelerated Soft-ISD post-processing recovers the full hqc-128 secret key from <10 seconds of online traces. "Constant-time C source" is provably insufficient — binary-level verification is required. [Link](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/693)

**4. Valsorda vs. Green: $5K Bet on Which Breaks First — ML-KEM-768 or X25519** — Filippo Valsorda and Matthew Green have staked $5K (arbiters: Schmieg, Virza, Ptacek) on whether lattice cryptanalysis or quantum computing poses the first practical threat before December 31, 2040. "Broken" = demonstrated practical attack recovering secrets on real hardware. Side-bettors have joined with $200-$10K stakes. This crystallizes the central PQ migration tension and sets a concrete, falsifiable bar for the hybrid-deployment debate. [Link](https://github.com/FiloSottile/ecc-vs-lattices-long-bet)

**5. DEX Perpetual Futures Hit $6.7T (+346% YoY)** — a16z reports DEX perp volume grew from $1.5T to $6.7T in 2025, with DEX share rising from 2.5% to 7.8% of the $86.2T total. RWA perps (stocks, commodities) comprised up to 44% of Hyperliquid's flow. HIP-3 enables permissionless perp market launches with 50% fee sharing. Onchain derivatives are no longer a crypto sideshow — with deep implications for MEV and order-flow dynamics as these volumes grow. [Link](https://a16zcrypto.substack.com/p/how-perpetual-futures-are-rewriting)

## Academic Papers

### MEV / Block Building

**[MEV-ACE: Identity-Authenticated Fair Ordering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07568)** — Wang
Slashable on-chain identity bonds + authenticated commit-and-open with validator receipt thresholds + verifiable delay randomness for ordering. Achieves order unpredictability, commitment authenticity, accountable inclusion. No threshold decryption committees required. Post-quantum compatible (ML-DSA 44). When bonds exceed potential MEV, sandwich attacks become economically irrational.

### Data Availability / Rollups

**[Proximity Signatures](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/694)** — Angeris, Gurkan (Bain Capital Crypto)
"Many-slot signatures" — signer is bound to produce exactly one valid signature per slot. Light nodes doing DAS verify both data integrity and signer authenticity from a small sample. BLS12-381: 48 bytes overhead per column. Complements FRIDA and EIP-4844. Closes the provenance gap in data availability sampling where erasure-coded data could pass proximity checks without authenticated origin.

**[Secure MSM Outsourcing for ZKP Generation](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/686)** — Xiong et al. (FC 2026)
Selectively outsources multi-scalar multiplication (the dominant ZK-SNARK bottleneck) to untrusted cloud GPUs. User blinds scalars to preserve witness confidentiality; verifies result with minimal overhead. Enables resource-constrained provers (mobile, IoT, light clients) to generate ZK proofs via decentralized prover marketplaces without revealing the witness.

**[Zeal: PIR for Non-Cooperative Databases](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/684)** — Zipkin, Dvir, Agrawal, Gupta, Homsi (UCSB)
First PIR scheme that works without database operator cooperation — no server-side crypto code required. Querier deploys unilaterally against any HTTP-accessible database. Enables private querying of blockchain explorers, analytics services, or any web DB. Paradigm shift: PIR moves from a bilateral protocol to a unilateral one. Relevant to MEV-aware privacy where users hide which transactions they inspect.

### Post-Quantum Cryptography

**[Breaking Optimized HQC via Cache-Timing](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/693)** — Dong, Guo
First full key-recovery via cache timing on any PQ cryptosystem. Compiler rewrites constant-time mask-based selection into secret-dependent control flow in the AVX2 HQC implementation. Flush+Reload + GPU-accelerated Soft-ISD recovers hqc-128 key from <10s of traces. "Constant-time source" is insufficient — binary-level verification mandatory.

**[Multivariate Adaptor Signatures for PQ Atomic Swaps](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/690)** — Meshram, Banerjee
First adaptor signatures from NIST PQC multivariate schemes (UOV/MAYO). Commitment-based approach achieves witness hiding and extractability under MQ-hardness. <0.4ms on Raspberry Pi 5 (UOV), up to 710 ops/s. Enables post-quantum atomic swaps and payment channels using NIST-standardized signatures on constrained devices.

**[PQ k-Times Traceable Ring Signature](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/697)** — Pareek, Gangopadhyay, Gangopadhyay
Lattice-based ring signature where signers are anonymous for up to k signatures under a tag, but the (k+1)-th deterministically reveals identity. Tunable privacy-accountability knob for governance, voting, and blockchain spam prevention. Post-quantum secure.

**[ML-KEM-768 vs. X25519 Long Bet](https://github.com/FiloSottile/ecc-vs-lattices-long-bet)** — Valsorda vs. Green
$5K public wager on which is broken first by 2040. Arbiters: Schmieg, Virza, Ptacek. "Broken" = practical attack on real hardware. Side bet ($1K) on significant ML-KEM weakening. Crystallizes whether the greater risk is lattice cryptanalysis or quantum computers arriving sooner.

### Distributed Systems / Agents

**[LogAct: Agentic Reliability via Shared Logs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07988)** — Balakrishnan, Bharambe, Testuggine et al.
Agents as deconstructed state machines over shared append-only logs. Every proposed action written before execution; pluggable "voter" modules inspect and optionally block. LLM-based semantic recovery over execution history replaces rigid checkpoint/replay. 3% benign utility cost for safety blocking. Enables auditable, pre-execution control for distributed agent deployments including security-sensitive operations.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

**ePBS difficulty is the honest signal about MEV's structural depth.** The EF's admission that enshrining PBS is "trickier than anticipated" — every stack layer must handle partial blocks and two-party coordination — is more informative than any research paper. Meanwhile, MEV-ACE proposes bypassing threshold decryption entirely via slashable identity bonds, Boneh's batch threshold encryption (last week) makes encrypted mempools practical, and Proximity Signatures close the DAS provenance gap. The MEV toolkit is expanding faster than the protocol can absorb it. Glamsterdam's ePBS will likely ship a minimal viable version rather than the full vision.

**PQ implementation security is failing at the compiler boundary.** The HQC cache-timing break demonstrates that NIST-standardized PQ code can be fully broken by a compiler optimization that silently removes constant-time guarantees. Combined with last week's Verification Facade (Hax pipeline passes insecure code through formal verification) and the prior SHA-3 fault injection results, a pattern is clear: the gap between provable-on-paper and secure-in-practice is widest at the boundaries — compiler output, verification translation, and hardware fault models. Binary-level verification is becoming a hard requirement for production PQ deployment.

**Agentic commerce is consolidating toward institutional players.** MRDN on x402 accounts for 85% of dollar volume from just 3 buyers. RelAI emerged last week as a wholesale facilitator. MPP's HYRE Agent API dominates with 9K txns but tiny dollar volume ($30). The ecosystem is bifurcating: high-frequency micro-payments for AI agent tooling (MPP's niche) vs. institutional-scale transfers flowing through a small number of whales (x402's emerging pattern). Seller-side contraction on x402 (502 → 290) suggests the long tail is not sustainable at current economics.

**The PQ migration risk is formally contested now.** The Valsorda-Green bet ($5K, public arbiters, 2040 deadline) puts real stakes behind the question of whether lattice cryptanalysis or quantum hardware poses the greater near-term threat. This isn't academic — the bet's structure ("practical attack on real hardware") is exactly the bar that should inform every hybrid-deployment decision. The HQC break and the sparse LWE phase transition (last week) show that both sides of the bet have live research ammunition.
